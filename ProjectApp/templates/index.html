How can I learn more?

Understand the data further.

Data is provided by the Centers for Disease Control's (CDC) and National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS), and it presents various information about the age-adjusted death rates for the ten leading causes of death in the United States beginning in 1999. Data are based on information from all resident death certificates filed in the 50 states and the District of Columbia using demographic and medical characteristics. Causes of death are classified by the International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision (ICD–10) and based on the underlying cause. For more information, visit NCHS.
